# Heenan Family Starter Kit

Use this document whenever you want to deploy the Heenan Family on a new
project, product, campaign, investigation, build, or overnight run.

The goal is to get from:

- "I have a thing I need to work on"

to:

- "the Family knows the mission, the stakes, the lanes, the rules, and
  how success will be judged"

---

## Step 1: Define the mission

Answer in one paragraph:

- What are we trying to build, fix, ship, learn, or decide?
- Why does it matter?
- What kind of result would make this feel like a real win?

Template:

```text
Mission:
We are trying to __________.
This matters because __________.
By the end, success looks like __________.
```

---

## Step 2: Define the stakes

Pick the 3-5 things that matter most on this project.

Examples:
- trust
- speed
- polish
- compliance
- originality
- revenue
- safety
- clarity
- scale

Template:

```text
Top stakes:
1. __________
2. __________
3. __________
4. __________
5. __________
```

---

## Step 4: Pick the operating pattern

Choose one:

### Strike Team
Use for urgent fixes or one concentrated objective.

### Overnight Build
Use for 4-8 hour autonomous work with multiple lanes.

### Research Room
Use when direction is unclear and evidence matters first.

### Polish Room
Use near launch when the work mostly exists and needs to feel right.

---

## Step 5: Assign the first wave

Do not assign everyone random work.
Assign the first 3-6 lanes that matter most.

Template:

```text
First wave:
- Heenan: __________
- Flair: __________
- Bockwinkel: __________
- Sting: __________
- Luger: __________
- Pillman: __________
- Windham: __________
- Hawk: __________
- Animal: __________
- Street: __________
- Raven: __________
- Hennig: __________
```

If someone is not needed in wave 1, leave them idle until their lane is
actually relevant.

---

## Step 6: Set the non-negotiables

You should always define:

- what is off-limits
- what counts as proof
- what counts as overclaiming
- when a final report is allowed
- what kind of work is considered drift

Template:

```text
Non-negotiables:
- Do not __________
- Do not __________
- Every completion claim needs __________
- Final report only after __________
- Drift includes __________
```

---

## Step 8: Define the final handoff

Recommended final structure:

1. What shipped
2. What was verified
3. What is only partially proven
4. What remains risky
5. What should happen next
6. Brutally honest summary
