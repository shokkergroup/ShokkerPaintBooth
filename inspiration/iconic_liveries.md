# Iconic Livery Archetypes

A field guide to legendary livery designs — described generically, without trademark content — and how to recreate their flavor in Shokker Paint Booth. Every entry lists the color scheme, design elements, era and venue, and an SPB recipe using bases, patterns, zones, and finishes.

Use this as an inspiration starting point. Pick an archetype that matches the energy of the car or series you are painting, then remix freely.

---

## 1. The White-and-Red Stripe (F1 Team Archetype)

One of the most celebrated F1 team looks of all time — a clean white base with a bold red longitudinal stripe running from nose to tail, and smaller red accents at the wings and airbox. Minimalist, confident, unmistakable from any angle.

**Color scheme:** Gloss white (85%), Rosso red (12%), Matte black accents (3%).

**Design elements:**
- Wide red stripe along the centerline of the car, nose to tail.
- Number roundel on the nose in a contrast color (often yellow or black).
- Small sponsor marks clustered on nose and wing endplates.
- Matte black driver frame around the cockpit.

**Era and venue:** Championship F1, late 90s through mid 2000s.

**SPB recipe:**
- Base: Gloss white (high-reflection clearcoat, spec B around 18).
- Add a centerline zone running the full length of the car, about 10–15% of the overall width. Apply solid red in that zone.
- Add small circular roundels on the front fender panels in the same red.
- Use the matte-black base in a narrow zone around the cockpit area.
- Finish: Gloss clearcoat everywhere except the cockpit accent.

## 2. Blue-and-Orange Classic (GT Racing Archetype)

One of the most recognized endurance-racing liveries in motorsport. A light powder-blue base bisected by a thick orange racing stripe running centerline from nose to tail. Numbers large and unmissable on both doors.

**Color scheme:** Powder blue (~70%), Vivid orange (~25%), White numbers on clean backgrounds (~5%).

**Design elements:**
- Full-length orange centerline stripe, constant width.
- Large white or black roundel on each door for the race number.
- Clean, uncluttered panels — this livery breathes.
- Sponsor marks sized to not disturb the stripe's rhythm.

**Era and venue:** Endurance GT racing, Le Mans, Daytona — 60s onward, revived many times.

**SPB recipe:**
- Base: Light blue gloss (slightly desaturated — not neon).
- Add a centerline stripe zone 15% of car width, painted in a saturated orange.
- Add white rectangular panels on both doors, each housing the race number in a chunky bold font.
- Finish: Gloss clearcoat for the wet, polished endurance look.

## 3. Tricolor Italian (Italian Team Archetype)

The Italian national tricolor — green, white, red — arranged as thin vertical bands on nose and rear fascia, combined with a deep red body. Elegant, historic, instantly Italian.

**Color scheme:** Rosso Italian red (~85%), Green, white, red tricolor accents (~10%), Chrome trim (~5%).

**Design elements:**
- Deep saturated red across the entire body.
- Thin tricolor bands at the nose leading edge and rear fascia.
- Chrome accents along trim lines.
- Small crest on each front fender.

**Era and venue:** Italian GT and sports car tradition, 60s to present.

**SPB recipe:**
- Base: Deep red gloss (lean toward the orange-red side, not crimson).
- Add three narrow vertical zones at the nose — green, white, red in order (left to right).
- Add matching tricolor bands at the rear fascia bottom edge.
- Apply chrome base to thin pinstripe zones along the belt line.
- Finish: Full gloss clearcoat with maximum shine (spec B=16).

## 4. Rainbow Sponsor Heritage (Cigarette-Era F1 Archetype)

One of F1's most colorful historic livery eras — a base of warm white broken by bands of red, yellow, and magenta arranged across the car's engine cover and sidepods. A festival on wheels.

**Color scheme:** Warm white (~55%), Red (~15%), Yellow (~15%), Magenta (~10%), Dark accents (~5%).

**Design elements:**
- White primary base.
- Red sweep across upper sidepod and engine cover.
- Yellow accent band.
- Magenta or pink accent band.
- All bands flow backward suggesting motion.

**Era and venue:** F1 from mid-70s through mid-2000s, before sponsor restrictions.

**SPB recipe:**
- Base: Gloss warm white.
- Add three flowing sweep zones across the upper body (use custom gradient or wave pattern).
- First zone: saturated red.
- Second zone: warm sunflower yellow.
- Third zone: saturated magenta.
- Finish: Gloss with slightly reduced pearl clarity (spec B=22).

## 5. Silver Arrow Heritage (German Silver Archetype)

Bare polished silver metal — or near-metal paint that reads as polished aluminum — across the entire body, with deep racing number roundels. Minimalist. Mechanical. Iconic.

**Color scheme:** Bright silver (~95%), Black or dark red roundel accents (~5%).

**Design elements:**
- Full-body silver with minimal or no decoration.
- Bold circular roundels for race numbers — often on the nose and flanks.
- Tricolor national bands at the nose tip (sometimes).

**Era and venue:** European grand prix racing, 30s and 50s; revived in modern F1.

**SPB recipe:**
- Base: Shokker silver or polished-aluminum base. Set spec R high (220+), G low (30–50), B max gloss (16).
- Add black circular roundels on the nose and both doors, sized large.
- Race number inside each roundel in chunky white or yellow font.
- Finish: High-reflection clearcoat.

## 6. British Racing Green Heritage

Deep forest-green body, understated, with a simple contrasting roundel for the race number and often a single thin yellow pinstripe around the nose opening. The gentleman-racer aesthetic.

**Color scheme:** British racing green (~90%), Contrasting roundel white or yellow (~7%), Pinstripe accent (~3%).

**Design elements:**
- Full-body deep green.
- Roundels for numbers — usually white with black numerals.
- Optional thin yellow pinstripe at the grille or nose intake edge.
- Chrome bumpers/trim where appropriate.

**Era and venue:** UK sports car racing from the 20s onward — Bentley, Jaguar, Lotus, Aston Martin tradition.

**SPB recipe:**
- Base: Deep British racing green (high saturation, blue-green side).
- Gloss finish with a slightly reduced clearcoat (spec B=20) for a classic less-plasticky look.
- Add white circular roundels on nose and doors.
- Add a narrow yellow pinstripe zone around the grille opening if present.

## 7. French Blue Heritage

A medium-saturation French-blue body, sometimes with a central white vertical stripe, frequently with a tricolor (blue, white, red) nose accent. Understated Gallic racing pride.

**Color scheme:** French blue (~75%), White stripe (~15%), Tricolor nose accents (~10%).

**Design elements:**
- Full-body French blue (slightly lighter than British racing green's equivalent position on the palette).
- Optional centerline white stripe.
- Tricolor nose band (blue/white/red left-to-right).

**Era and venue:** French sports car and grand prix tradition from the 20s through present.

**SPB recipe:**
- Base: French-blue gloss (medium saturation, medium brightness).
- Optional: narrow white centerline stripe zone running hood to rear deck.
- Tricolor nose zone — three thin vertical bands at the leading edge.
- Finish: Gloss with classic clearcoat.

## 8. Japanese Red/White Championship

A white base with a large bold red circular disc on the hood and doors — simple, graphic, symbolic of Japanese racing heritage.

**Color scheme:** Gloss white (~75%), Saturated red (~20%), Black number or lettering (~5%).

**Design elements:**
- Clean white primary.
- Large red circles on hood and door panels.
- Race number centered in each red circle in black or white.

**Era and venue:** Japanese touring and GT racing from the 70s onward.

**SPB recipe:**
- Base: Gloss white.
- Add circular zones on hood and both doors, filled with saturated red.
- Race number centered in each circle.
- Finish: High-gloss clearcoat.

---

## 9. American Muscle Blue/White Stripes

Deep royal blue body with two thick parallel white stripes running hood-to-deck down the centerline. All-American muscle-car swagger.

**Color scheme:** Royal blue (~65%), White stripes (~30%), Chrome accents (~5%).

**Design elements:**
- Full-body deep royal blue base.
- Two parallel white stripes, each about 10% of body width, with a narrow gap between.
- Chrome bumpers and trim.
- Optional pinstripe outline around each white stripe in light blue or silver.

**Era and venue:** American muscle-car racing and street scene, 60s onward.

**SPB recipe:**
- Base: Royal blue gloss.
- Add two parallel centerline stripe zones running hood to rear deck. Each 10% body width, gap of 2% between them.
- Finish: Gloss clearcoat, spec B=16.
- Optional: thin silver pinstripe zones outlining the white stripes.

## 10. Matte Black Modern Stealth

A full-body matte black finish. No stripes. No decoration. Sometimes a single small badge or serial number on one fender. Menacing, expensive, silent.

**Color scheme:** Matte black (~98%), One small accent (~2%).

**Design elements:**
- Full-body matte finish.
- Blacked-out wheels, grille, trim.
- Optional single small badge — often in gunmetal or dark chrome.

**Era and venue:** Modern custom scene, high-end aftermarket, 2010s onward.

**SPB recipe:**
- Base: Matte black. Set spec R=0, G=220, B=15 (very dull clearcoat, high roughness, non-metallic).
- No other colors.
- Optional: small zone on a single front fender with a gunmetal or dark-chrome badge.
- Finish: Matte clearcoat — keep the B channel high to kill reflection.

## 11. Chrome Gold Show Car

Full-body polished gold-chrome finish. Reads as solid gold from every angle. Maximum ostentation.

**Color scheme:** Chrome gold (~100%).

**Design elements:**
- Polished chrome-gold body.
- Often no numbers or sponsor marks — the gold is the statement.
- Optional: diamond-encrusted-look accents via pattern overlay.

**Era and venue:** Custom show scene, tuner displays, pop-culture music videos.

**SPB recipe:**
- Base: Chrome-gold base. Set spec R=255, G=0, B=16 (max metallic, max smooth, max gloss).
- Apply across every zone.
- Finish: The chrome base handles the look — clearcoat goes on transparent gloss.

## 12. Pastel 80s

Salmon pink, teal, pale yellow, and mint — arranged in geometric blocks across the body. The Miami nightclub of liveries.

**Color scheme:** Salmon pink (~30%), Teal (~25%), Pale yellow (~20%), Mint (~15%), White (~10%).

**Design elements:**
- Geometric color-blocked zones with clean hard edges.
- Optional triangular or angular graphic accents.
- Palm silhouette decals (optional).

**Era and venue:** 80s — Miami, LA, pop culture, Lamborghini club scene.

**SPB recipe:**
- Four primary zones — hood (pink), door (teal), rear quarter (mint), accent bands (yellow).
- Add triangular graphic accents in a contrast color (often white or pastel purple).
- Finish: Gloss clearcoat with spec B=16.

---

## 13. Neon 90s Tuner

Electric lime green or neon magenta body, optional zigzag graphics, large sponsor text across the sides — the import tuner aesthetic.

**Color scheme:** Neon base (~60%), Black accents (~25%), Chrome or silver accents (~15%).

**Design elements:**
- Highly saturated neon primary.
- Zigzag, splash, or tribal graphics on the side panels.
- Large branded text across the side of the car.
- Chrome or silver accent stripes.

**Era and venue:** 90s and early 2000s import tuner scene.

**SPB recipe:**
- Base: Electric neon green or magenta. Push saturation to near maximum.
- Add zigzag or splash pattern zones on each door.
- Add silver pinstripe outlines.
- Finish: Gloss clearcoat, spec B=16.

---

## 14. Weathered Barn Find

Faded original paint with rust streaks, dust patina, and faded sponsor markings. The "survivor" aesthetic — proudly un-restored.

**Color scheme:** Original faded primary (~60%), Rust and oxidation overlay (~20%), Dust beige accumulation (~10%), Old sponsor remnants (~10%).

**Design elements:**
- Original paint color, but desaturated and faded.
- Rust patches at panel edges, around wheel arches, along lower rocker.
- Dust and dirt texture across lower body panels.
- Faded, peeling sponsor decals.

**Era and venue:** Vintage-tribute builds, resto-mods, art installations.

**SPB recipe:**
- Base: Original body color, desaturated (pull saturation down 40%).
- Apply SPB's weathering/damage pattern overlay — rust along rocker and arches.
- Apply dust texture pattern on lower body panels.
- Add faded sponsor text (reduce opacity or pair with distressed pattern).
- Finish: Matte clearcoat (spec B=240) for dead patina.

---

## 15. Flat Military Transport

Flat olive drab, no markings except small serial numbers and unit insignia. Purely utilitarian.

**Color scheme:** Olive drab (~95%), Flat white serial number (~3%), Tan or foliage gray accents (~2%).

**Design elements:**
- Full-body matte olive drab.
- Small white or black serial numbers on front fender and rear quarter.
- Optional small unit insignia on door.
- No gloss anywhere.

**Era and venue:** Military-themed tribute builds, off-road racers, art cars.

**SPB recipe:**
- Base: Olive drab paint (desaturated dark yellow-green).
- Matte finish — spec R=0, G=230, B=20.
- Small white serial numbers on front fender and rear quarter.
- Optional small square zone on the door with a unit insignia graphic.

## Composing Your Own Livery from These Archetypes

These 15 archetypes are flexible starting points. Mix-and-match freely:

- Take the **Silver Arrow base** and add the **Tricolor Italian nose**.
- Take the **Blue-and-Orange GT stripe** and swap in **British Racing Green** on either side of the stripe.
- Take the **Matte Black Stealth** base and add a single **Neon 90s Tuner** zigzag bolt.

The archetypes above pull directly from motorsport history; your remix is new. That's how livery design has worked for a century.

When you create something you love, share it back to the SPB community via the discord or league showcases.
